Giraffe Central is your guide to all things giraffe!

A giraffe is a large fleet African ruminant mammal (Giraffa camelopardalis) that is the tallest of living quadrupeds and has a very long neck and a short coat with dark blotches separated by pale lines (Mirriam-Webster)

A hoofed animal, distingushed by their distintive coats, horn-like ossicones, and long neck. Currently, there are four extant species of giraffe, all told apart based on their coat patterns. There are seven extinct species of Giraffe.

  1. Northern Giraffe - Giraffa camelopardalis
  2. Reticulated Giraffe - Giraffa reticulata
  3. Masai Giraffe - Giraffa tippelskirchi
  4. Southern Giraffe - Giraffa giraffa
